BuddyFind is a mobile application that helps people with mental health concerns to find a pet companion and connect with the community who shares the similar experiences.


Role: End-to-end UX Designer

Type: Individual Project

Duration: Sep - Dec 2019

Tool: Adobe XD, Illustrator, Photoshop






Challenge


For the past 6 years, I've witnessed how my close family suffered from depression. The years of medication and therapist treatment didn't help much, so I wanted to find an alternative and healthy way to help him as well as other people who suffer from mental health disease.




Final Solution Overview







Customized Match


Take a 5-10 minutes short questionnaire to find the best match possible. Partnership with the local training programs and shelters, BuddyFind helps users to match with pets that have different personalities and level of experiences based on usersâ€™ answers to the questionnaire.







Meet-and-Greet Session


Differing from conventional adoption process, pets are brought to users safely by professional staffs. Users feel free to choose locations they prefer to meet with the pets. During the meet-and-greet session, users are able to interact with the pets under instruction of the staff and decide to proceed the adoption process..







Connect with the Community


BuddyFind also provides a platform where users are able to connect with each other who are close by. Volunteers actively organize offline events and encourage users to use the forum to share their feelings and thoughts, ask for help or chat with each other.

Design Process




Research


Before diving into the user research, I did some preliminary research on current and alternative treatment on depression and other mental health diseases. And here is what I found:

  Taking medication and visiting therapist are the main treatments to depression.
  Animal-assisted-therapy is not considered as medical treatment but it has positive and long-lasting impact on patients.
   When the patients take medications for long period of time, the medication will likely become less effective.

My initial research showed that the animal-assisted therapy is a great alternative of medical treatments, therefore I decided to do user research on benefits of having pets and how pets change lives of people who have mental health concern.


Interview


To begin with, I wanted to conduct interviews to validate my hypothesis of having pets is good for mental health.




Key Insights:

 Most participants adopted their pets at the shelter/adoption center but the process was stressful.
 Participants were aware of animal-assisted therapy but the resources are very limited.
 Most participants felt less stressful when having a pet.
 Some participants are family members who went through the whole adoption process because people who have server mental health disease are not likely to go outside.




Competitive Analysis


Based on the interview results, I learned that there is not enough resources for people with mental health concern to find a pet companion, but I still want to do some research on how the current available resources provide help to people in needs. Here are some key findings:

  The process to adopt an emotional support animal is complicated.
  The pet adoption apps on the market doesn't fulfill the needs of targeted users.
  Animal-assisted therapy is mostly available in medical institution and is limited to hospitalized patients.

Design Changes


After transferring design concepts to paper prototype, I conducted usability test to test out the main features of the prototype. Based on the feedback from the users, I made some changes to my low-fidelity prototype.

1. Some users were confused about what the meet-and-greet session is about and what to do during the session. I added a brief to-do-list after scheduling the appointment which informs the users what to expect for the session.



2. Users mentioned that mental health issue could be a sensitive topic which some people don't feel comfortable to talk about, so the use of words should be carefully chosen. Instead of directly asking the name of their condition, I frame the question to ask about their feelings.



3. "What happens after people adopt their pets?" was the question I got the most during the usability test. There could be a variety of problems that users don't know where to seek help which could be additional stress to them. Therefore, I added the community feature where pet owners could connect with each other.

Takeaway


This project guided me through the entire process of a UX project. I learned the importance of testing early and frequently. Keep engaging with users really helped me to stay focused on users' needs and validate my hypothesis from early stage of the process.

Mental health is one of the social issues that I concern about the most. Due to the small number of targeted users I could interview and test, for the next step, I would like to find more targeted users so I could better understand their needs and further refine my design.
