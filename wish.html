Work




Redesigned the the browsing screen of Wish mobile app to help provide a more personalized and curated shopping experience.

Overview:

This is a one week design challenge as part of the interview process for Wish product design internship. The prompt was to design a solution for one problem found in the Wish mobile app.

Duration:

3 days

Tool:

Adobe XD, illustrator, Photoshop

Skills:

Mobile design, visual design, rapid prototyping, user testing




RESEARCH

Understanding users and value



To better understand the target users and product value of Wish, I started doing some company research by reading online articles and also the talk of Peter Szulczewski, the CEO and Co-Founder of Wish, on Youtube. Based on the preliminary research, I obtained a general overview of Wish's target population and Wish's product values.

DISCOVER

Explore as a user



To better understand the problem, I started exploring the app as a new user and found out it was hard for me to navigate through the application and the contents are not curated. In order to validate my observations, I decided to ask other user's browsing experience.










INITIAL PROBLEM

How do users think of Wish's browsing experience when navigating through the app?

USER TESTING

Hard to find interested items



I conducted user testing with 5 new users of Wish to explore the app and see if they can find any items that interests them. The user testing results showed that most of users were not able to find items that interest them.


- "The category is all over the place. I didn't expect it to appear on the top navigation. "



- "I had to click into each item to see its review and product name which is not convenient."



- "The filter doesn't have too many options to choose from."









DESIGN OBJECTIVE

Improve the browsing experience by redesigning the item card and category, providing more curated and personalized content.

SOLUTION

Personalized Category and restructured item card



Based on the user research, I restructured the information on the item card so users are able to get a overview of the products such as name of the product and product reviews without clicking into each product. To help users find their interests more easily, I also redesigned the top navigation menu so users don't need to scroll horizontally and all the categories are contained under one tab. To make the navigation more consistent, I unified the style of the icons and text so users could better understand what information they expect to see when they click the tab.



Ideation


I started by ideating some solutions based on the design objective. As a result, I decided to go with version 2 for two reasons: 1) I wanted to simplify the navigation while maintaining the original design style. 2) The first version added extra clicks to select user's interests.






Wireframing







Design Changes and User Testing





Before proceeding to create a high-fidelity mockup, I conducted 1 user testing to get feedback on the redesigned browsing experience. The user mentioned that after the categories are applied, since the content is organized by its category it might take awhile to scroll to the second category.

When creating the final design, I added a shuffle switch where users can turn on to shuffle the content and browse a mix of different items. If users would prefer to see items within one category, they can turn the switch off.

REFLECTION

Takeaway and next step



Through this design challenge, I learned what Wish's values are and the current browsing experience of Wish mobile app. By exploring user pain points and different design iterations, I wanted to help users to easily and effectively find items that might interest them. Having a better curated contents and shopping categories could help to users to make better buying decision and also promote a better navigational experiences which aligns with the company's value.


For next steps, I would like to spend more time on user testing and also take a deeper dive into other problem areas such as ineffective filtering feature and communication between sellers and buyers.
