Pals with Paws integrates bite-sized break to physically move and interact between virtual classes, re-energizing children by reducing screen fatigue and providing sense of purpose.

Type:

Product Development Team Project

Duration:

Fall 2020 (3 months)

My Role:

I led the UX research and design process with a Project Manager, an Engineer, a Product Designer and an MBA.

Skills:

Concept Testing, Product Development, Prototyping, UX/UI Design, Website Design, Usability Testing




CHALLENGE

Learning during pandemic



As the pandemic emerges, it shifts the way of children learning from having lessons in classrooms to learning in the virtual environment. The feeling of isolation from friends at school and increased screen time became major pain points of children and concerns of parents.

How might we help children ages 6-9 years reduce screen time and make time for activities outside the classroom to get moving and reduce feelings of loneliness?

SOLUTION

Take a screen break with pals with paws



A cuddly toy that connects with the application to help elementary schoolers reduce screen time and feelings of loneliness. Pals with Paws seamlessly introduces short breaks to move and interact between virtual classes.

01

Choose your own pal



Before purchase, you can choose one of three pals, answer user questions so the application helps to recommend personalized activities, and import your class schedule to get notification that fit seamlessly into your virtual school day.



02

Connect your pal with the app



When you receive your pal from the mail, scan the QR code on the back of the collar of your pal to register your pal and connect it with the app.



03

Get up and move with your pal



Take a break from virtual learning and complete daily challenge to unlock the next level. Pals with paws also encourages friendly competition between users; you can give high-fives to cheer for your friend's achievement.



04

Play with your friends



A variety of team challenges are available for you to take on with your friends. Pals with paws allows you to interact with others even though you are apart from each other.



05

Customize your pal



You can unlock virtual accessories to customize your pal by completing different challenges.

HOW WE GOT THERE

Diverge and converge



Competing with other 5 teams in a virtual market space, we went through the product development cycle of identifying user needs and market potential, brainstorming design concepts, creating physical and digital prototype, validating designs with users, and launching the product in a virtual trade show.





RESEARCH

How pandemic changes people's lives?



Based on the project prompt of reducing isolation and enhancing social engagement during pandemic, we started listening to people's stories and collecting insights on how people's lives have changed since the pandemic started. We distilled some major pain points from our interviews and observations:



  Missing physical interaction

  Too much screen time caused screen fatigue

  Have trouble keeping daily routine

  Not able to interact with friends





TARGET USERS

Design for young generation



From the analysis of the preliminary interview, we were surprised by the insights from some of our interviewees who are parents and educators. They addressed that many parents are concerned about children not being able to focus due to increased screen time and to hang out with their friends at school. After some secondary research on this topic, we were curious about what do children think of learning remotely and decided to conduct more targeted interviews with children from age 6 to 9.

We conducted remote interviews with 7 elementary school students, 2 parents and 2 school teachers and further confirmed the earlier assumptions of two challenges elementary school students are facing. 1. IDEATION

Brainstorm with user needs in mind



With the 2 user needs in mind, I led a 30-minutes remote brainstorming session with the team, combined the ideas that share similar goals and evaluated what ideas better fulfill user's needs. We ended up having 3 leading concepts.













WHAT DO USERS THINK

No more screen time



37

quantitative survey

5

conduct remote qualitative concept testing with target users



We conducted both quantitative and qualitative concept testing on our 3 leading concepts and 3 existing products as benchmark. The concept testing tells us the willingness of users to purchase each product. Among all the 6 product concepts presented, pals with paws (originally "My Pet") became the most popular concept. The key takeaways from the concept testing are:


1. Both parents and children, tend to be averse to introducing more screen time but have difficulty envisioning ways to collaborate from afar.


2. Children tend to respond to rewards systems as motivation and sense of accomplishment and momentum.


3. Social benefits are valued, but not enough. Products need to provide additional value, such as responsibility, fitness or mindfulness goals.

"It would be great to minimize the screen time and maximize movement,while not adding new tech to the home." - From a parent

Going tangible



Knowing the design goal and concept, we started to pay attention to the technical feasibility and the details of the physical product. Led by the team's main developer Sean Geier, we began our iterative prototyping process by using cardboard, 3D modeling, 3D printing and Arduino components.













App design



I took on the sole responsibility to design the application. Based on the early feedback from the users, most of children at the age of 6-9 don't have a mobile phone, rather they have school tablet or computer. Therefore, we decided to design tablet application. Before diving into the design, I started by studying the design pattern of children's app and collecting some app examplars so I can better understand how children engage with technology. And then I created digital prototypes and iterated based on user testing.



















FURTHER VALIDATION

User testing and conjoint analysis



To get more concrete feedback on the app feature and usability, we conducted 2 remote user testings with a 9-year-old and a 8-year-old student. I designed 5 tasks for them complete and iterated the design based on their feedback. We also conducted conjoint analysis to help evaluate the cost of the pals with paws and needs of certain features of the physical product.

FUTURE

Independent from the virtual world



We are the "Most Profitable Product" among the 6 teams in the final trade show. During the 3-months journey, we had lots of help from design/product development specialists and professors and we also received great advices for further development. One of the them is to make pals with paws independent from the virtual world. Even though we already tried to minimize the screen time, pals with paws still depends on the digital space where users need to interact with. So the next step would be how can we further develop the product so users only needs to interact with the toy rather than a tablet?





TAKEAWAY

As the sole designer in the team, I learned how to collaborate with the engineer and project managers and how to lead design in the team. Even though remote collaboration adds challenge to the project, I learned how to convey my designs more effectively within a limited time and space. Design is collaborative work. We were able to achieve our final design because of different perspectives and values from diverse fields contributed to the project.
