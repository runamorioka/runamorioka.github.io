Working with LoanSense, an Ann Arbor-based startup that helps students with loan management, my team evaluated its B2C product interface and provided design recommendations based on the user research and usability study findings.


Role: UX Researcher and designer

Skill: Interview, Survey, Heuristic Evaluation, Usability Testing

Type: Client-based UX Research Project

Duration: Jan - April 2020




Disclaimer: Subjected to NDA, I am not able to disclose the final findings and recommendations. For detailed information, please email me.




Challenge


As LoanSense opened up its free loan consultation service to encourage people, who are eligible for the Public Service Loan Forgiveness Program (PSLF), to enroll their plans, the company realizes that people are signing up for the consultation but the enrollment rate is low. Over the three months, our team utilized various research methods to evaluate the usability of the LoanSense consultation feature.



Process



Interaction Map


We started by understanding the current user flow, identifying the main functions of the consultation service, and marking flows that may cause a usability issue.





Interview


After having a general grasp of the user flow, we interviewed 4 people who are eligible for PSLF to better understand target users' pain points and needs during the repayment process.





Comparative Analysis


To better evaluate the usability of LoanSense interface, we wanted to know what features or services other loan management tools have. We chose eight online loan management services and generated a comparative matrix. The criteria of the matrix was created based on our client's goal and user's needs that we synthesized from the interviews. We also created visual analysis to see how these loan management websites positioned themselves.






Survey






Heuristic Evaluation



We conducted a heuristic evaluation of the onboarding process and repayment plan recommendation page of LoanSense's B2C product interface based on Nielson's 10 Usability Heuristics rules and Nielsen severity scale to assess the impact and prioritize the issues uncovered.




Usability Testing


Based on the heuristic evaluation, we were able to identify some usability issues of LoanSense free consultation feature. We wanted to conduct usability testing in order to validate our evaluation findings. Total of 5 participants participated the virtual usability-testing. For each usability test, each group member was the moderator and the logger for at least one test. We used Zoom to conduct and record our usability tests. We created 6 tasks for participants to test on and identified some usability issues based on the success criteria.



6 tasks for usability testing




Takeaway


The importance of UX research


Working on the real-world problem for clients, I learned how UX research plays an important role in the design process. A thorough user research guided us to a set of clear findings and comprehensive quantitative/qualitative data which helped us to stay focus on user's pain points and not make decisions based on our assumptions.
